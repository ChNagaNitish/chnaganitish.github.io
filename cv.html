Academic Projects

Numerical investigation of Scramjet combustor

Defence Research and Development Laboratory

Details: A generic scramjet combustor is taken from the literature and validated the CFD simulations against the experiment. An optimization study is taken up for the scramjet combustor by considering the design parameters as the angle of fuel injection and the injector diameter. The objective is to maximize thrust and combustion efficiency and minimize total pressure loss. The equivalence ratio is maintained and hence the pressure of fuel injection increases as the injector diameter is reduced. For the CFD simulations, ANSYS CFX is used. Various flow features were observed. After simulating all the cases, the configuration with better thrust is proposed.

A Quasi-one-dimensional model for scramjet combustor with wall injector

Defence Research and Development Laboratory

Details: A quasi-one-dimensional solver is constructed in Python for Scramjet combustor with wall injection and validated the same against two different combustor geometries with different experimental setup. The results show reasonable accuracy where the assumptions of the model hold good.

Numerical Simulation for the Flow Characteristics over a Double Forward Facing Step

Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham

Details: The simulations are carried out in ANSYS Fluent. The distance between the two forward steps are varied and the flow pattern is observed. The Reynolds number and distance between two steps have significant effect on separation length and skin friction coefficient after the first step.

Qualitative Nonlinear Analysis of Ship Dynamics

Sreenidhi Institute of Science and Technology

Details: A mathematical model for a sea and a ferry are taken from literature. The stability of the ferry in the sea is observed, by using perturbation theory, for different initial conditions of sea and ferry. MATLAB is used for the analysis.

Numerical study on Airfoils

Sreenidhi Institute of Science and Technology

Details: Using ANSYS Fluent, the external flow over airfoil structures are analyzed by varying the camber and thickness of airfoil and angle of attack. Stalling effect is observed at high angle of attack.
